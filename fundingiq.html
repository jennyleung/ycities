Middle and High School Students

Youth FundingIQ Intensive

Learn how investors actually think — building the judgment and decision skills behind every funding "yes," "no," or "not yet."

Home› Educational Programs› FundingIQ Intensive

About This Program

In the innovation ecosystem, founders are only one side of the equation. FundingIQ invites motivated middle and high school students to explore entrepreneurship from a different vantage point — the investor's lens. Rather than focusing on how to pitch, students learn how decisions are made: what makes a venture compelling, why strong ideas don't always receive funding, and how investors weigh risk, evidence, and potential.

[Photo of middle/high school students engaged in group discussion — analyzing business scenarios together in a virtual or classroom setting]

What Students Learn

This 3-week virtual intensive emphasizes judgment, reasoning, and pattern recognition — not financial modeling — making sophisticated decision frameworks accessible to curious and ambitious learners earlier in their journey.

  • How investors evaluate early-stage ventures
  • Why strong ideas don't always receive funding
  • How to weigh risk, evidence, and potential
  • The logic behind a "yes," a "no," or a "not yet"
  • Sharper critical thinking and strategic instincts
  • A deeper understanding of how innovation moves from idea to opportunity

Program Format

3 weeks, fully virtual — structured sessions with application exercises. Designed for curious and ambitious learners who are ready to think differently about entrepreneurship.

Prerequisite Middle or high school students with prior exposure to entrepreneurship (coursework, school program, or personal project).
Optional Pathway A Mentor Certification pathway is available for eligible participants who complete the FundingIQ Intensive and wish to contribute to the Youth Venture Mentoring Network.
