Youth Venture Mentoring Network

A structured next step where young innovators validate ideas, reduce risk, and gain traction with guided, near-peer mentorship.

About This Program

The Youth Venture Mentoring (YVM) Network supports students who want to continue building their ventures after a class or program. Through structured virtual mentoring, milestone tracking, and supervised near-peer coaching, participants focus on validation, traction, and sustainable models — while receiving feedback grounded in an investor perspective.

[Photo of a student and near-peer mentor in a virtual mentoring session — reviewing a business plan or prototype together via video call]

How It Works

Students work in a structured virtual environment with near-peer mentors who have completed yCITIES programs. Sessions focus on practical progress: defining assumptions, gathering evidence, and iterating on the venture model. Milestone tracking keeps participants accountable and moving forward.

  • Structured virtual mentoring sessions
  • Near-peer coaching from certified youth mentors
  • Milestone-based progress tracking
  • Feedback grounded in an investor perspective
  • Focus on validation, traction, and sustainable models

What You Can Bring

Students can bring any of the following:

  • A past program venture they want to continue developing
  • A new idea different from their Demo Day project
  • An existing small business they want to learn how to scale
Prerequisite Middle or high school students with prior entrepreneurship exposure (coursework, school program, or personal project).
